# Setup Taxonomy

Named list of trade setups you actually use. Grows organically. Each
setup should be describable in one sentence such that a stranger could
tag a chart as "this is one" or "this is not."

Once you've logged 20+ trades per setup, you have data on which setups
actually make money. That's the reverse-engineered intuition.

## Format

- **setup-slug** — one-sentence definition. _Optional: link to a chart image._

## Current taxonomy

<!-- Add entries here as you notice them. Slug in kebab-case.
     Don't invent setups you haven't traded — add each when it first fires. -->

- **range-breakout-vol-dryup** — tightening N-day range on declining volume, breakout above the range on above-average volume
- **pullback-to-rising-20ma** — trend intact (price > 50MA > 200MA); pulls back to touch or briefly pierce the rising 20-day MA on lighter volume, then reclaims
- **failed-breakdown** — price breaks below support, immediately reverses back above within 1-2 bars; often traps shorts
- **gap-and-go** — pre-market gap on news, opens near high of day, holds first 15-min pullback, resumes uptrend
- **mean-revert-oversold** — RSI(14) < 30 on a stock in an uptrend; entry on first close back above 30; stop under recent swing low
